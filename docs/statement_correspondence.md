# Statement Correspondence

This note classifies the correspondence between informal statements and their
representations in the current Lean source. The relation labels mean:

- `exact`: the assumptions and conclusion agree, up to differences in notation
  and type encoding;
- `representation`: the mathematical content agrees, but Lean makes some data
  or an encoding explicit;
- `stronger`: Lean is more general in the dimension named in the note;
- `weaker`: Lean proves less than the informal statement;
- `derived`: a convenient declaration combines several source results;
- `totalized`: Lean quantifies over all values of the natural-number
  parameters, including degenerate cases outside the classical range;
- `not formalized`: the informal object or identification has not been
  formalized in the current artifact.

These labels do not express a global comparison. A declaration may, for
example, be stronger in its coefficient field or term-order parameter while
using an explicit coefficient normalization or a different representation of
the ideal.

## Informal/formal mismatches

| Topic | Informal source | Current Lean artifact | Relation and consequence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ideal and geometry | Sturmfels denotes the vanishing ideal of the rank-`≤ r` matrix variety by `I_r`. | The development and accompanying manuscript use the local symbol `J_r`; `Jr` is definitionally equal to the ideal generated by `(r + 1) × (r + 1)` generic minors. | The affine rank variety, its vanishing ideal, and their identification with this generated ideal are `not formalized`. `Jr` must not be described as an already formalized vanishing ideal or as Sturmfels’s notation. |
| Coefficient field | The source theorem is stated over a field of characteristic zero. | The main theorem assumes only `[Field k]`. | This is `stronger` with respect to the coefficient field: the Lean theorem holds over fields of arbitrary characteristic. The current theorem is not stated over arbitrary commutative rings. |
| Parameter range | The classical nondegenerate range has `r < m,n`. | The declarations quantify all `m n r : ℕ`. | `totalized`: when `(r + 1)` exceeds a matrix dimension, the minor index type or generator set may be empty and the result may degenerate to a trivial case. This is not an unqualified strict strengthening. |
| Term order | A concrete lexicographic order induced by the paper’s variable-order convention. | The main theorem applies to every `ord` satisfying `IsAntidiagonalTermOrder`; `antiDiagonalLex` encodes increasing rows and decreasing columns within rows and is proved to satisfy the predicate. | The abstract theorem is `stronger` in the order parameter. The concrete order is a `representation`; the artifact does not prove it definitionally identical to all source leading-order conventions. |
| Reducedness and normalization | A reduced Gröbner basis, with nonzero scalar normalization left implicit at the coefficient level. | `GrPlusOne` is proved to be a Gröbner basis and to satisfy the interreduction condition. `normalizedGrPlusOne`, obtained by multiplying every minor by the inverse of its anti-diagonal leading coefficient, is proved to be a reduced Gröbner basis under the library’s monic definition. | `representation`. The reducedness component is formalized, but normalization is explicit in Lean: the original minors may have reverse-permutation leading coefficient `-1`, whereas each normalized minor is monic. Both families generate `Jr` and have the same leading exponents. |
| Leading object | Paper prose uses an initial monomial. | `ord.degree p`, `ord.leadingCoeff p`, `ord.leadingTerm p`, and `MvPolynomial.monomial E 1` are distinct. | `representation`. Reverse-permutation signs are units over a field, so signed leading terms and coefficient-one monomials generate the same ideal; they are not definitionally equal. |
| Grading | The source `deg` records the row and column sums as multidegree data, or content. | The public Hilbert-function chain uses total degree `Finsupp.degree` and homogeneous components. `MinorIndex` and `MinorWord` provide row- and column-content APIs, which the local straightening code uses, but the final Hilbert count is based on total degree. | The current proof interface is a total-degree specialization rather than a word-for-word `exact` correspondence with the multigraded statement. |
| Straightening | Proposition-level straightening plus its support information. | Filtered existence, degree support, length support, and polynomial-level linear independence are proved separately; `straightening_law` combines them into a unique expansion. | `derived`. A separate proof of linear independence prevents circularity in the uniqueness argument. |

The public straightening declarations assume `[Field k]`. This is more general
in characteristic than Sturmfels’s characteristic-zero setting, but it is not
the full arbitrary-commutative-ring coefficient scope available for some
classical Doubilet–Rota–Stein straightening identities.

## Limitations

The current artifact does not formalize:

- the affine rank-`≤ r` matrix variety;
- its vanishing ideal or its geometric identification with `Jr`;
- the complete multigraded Hilbert-function statement from row and column
  content (the public final chain is total-degree graded);
- a public straightening API over arbitrary commutative rings.

These are current limitations, not claims of implemented functionality.
