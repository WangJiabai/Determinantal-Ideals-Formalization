# Statement Correspondence

This note classifies how informal statements are represented by the current
Lean source. The relation labels mean:

- `exact`: assumptions and conclusion agree, apart from notation and type
  encoding;
- `representation`: the mathematics agrees but Lean exposes explicit data or
  an encoding;
- `stronger`: Lean is more general in the dimension named in the note;
- `weaker`: Lean proves less than the informal statement;
- `derived`: a convenient declaration combines several source results;
- `totalized`: Lean quantifies all natural-number parameters, including
  degenerate cases outside the classical range;
- `not formalized`: the informal object or identification is absent.

No label should be read as a global comparison: a declaration may be stronger
in its coefficient field and weaker because it omits reducedness.

## Informal/formal mismatches

| Topic | Informal source | Current Lean artifact | Relation and consequence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ideal and geometry | Sturmfels denotes the rank-`≤ r` matrix-variety vanishing ideal by `I_r`. | The development and accompanying manuscript use the local symbol `J_r`; `Jr` is definitionally the ideal generated by `(r + 1) × (r + 1)` generic minors. | The affine rank variety, its vanishing ideal, and their identification with this generated ideal are `not formalized`. `Jr` must not be described as an already formalized vanishing ideal or as Sturmfels’s notation. |
| Coefficient field | The stated source version uses a characteristic-zero field. | The main theorem assumes only `[Field k]`. | `stronger` in this dimension: characteristic-unrestricted over fields. It is not a theorem over arbitrary commutative rings. |
| Parameter range | The classical nondegenerate range has `r < m,n`. | The declarations quantify all `m n r : ℕ`. | `totalized`: when `(r + 1)` exceeds a matrix dimension, the minor index type or generator set may be empty and the result may degenerate to a trivial case. This is not an unqualified strict strengthening. |
| Term order | A concrete lexicographic order induced by the paper’s variable-order convention. | The main theorem accepts every `ord` satisfying `IsAntidiagonalTermOrder`; `antiDiagonalLex` encodes increasing rows and decreasing columns within rows and is proved to satisfy the predicate. | The abstract theorem is `stronger` in the order parameter. The concrete order is a `representation`; the artifact does not prove it definitionally identical to all source leading-order conventions. |
| Reducedness | A reduced Gröbner basis. | Only `IsGroebnerBasis` is proved. | `weaker`. Reducedness requires normalizing every minor by its leading coefficient, proving that every non-leading monomial of each normalized minor is not divisible by the leading monomials of the other generators, and connecting these conditions to a formal reducedness definition/API in the Gröbner library. |
| Leading object | Paper prose uses an initial monomial. | `ord.degree p`, `ord.leadingCoeff p`, `ord.leadingTerm p`, and `MvPolynomial.monomial E 1` are distinct. | `representation`. Reverse-permutation signs are units over a field, so signed leading terms and coefficient-one monomials generate the same ideal; they are not definitionally equal. |
| Grading | The source `deg` records row and column sums as a multidegree/content. | The public Hilbert-function chain uses total degree `Finsupp.degree` and homogeneous components. `MinorIndex`, `MinorWord`, and local straightening code do have row/column content APIs, used to control straightening, but the final Hilbert count is total-degree based. | A total-degree specialization/current proof interface, not a word-for-word `exact` correspondence to the multigraded statement. |
| Straightening | Proposition-level straightening plus its support information. | Filtered existence, degree support, length support, and polynomial linear independence are separate; `straightening_law` combines them into unique expansion. | `derived`. Independent linear independence prevents a circular uniqueness argument. |

The public straightening declarations assume `[Field k]`. This is more general
in characteristic than Sturmfels’s characteristic-zero setting, but it is not
the full arbitrary-commutative-ring coefficient scope available for some
classical Doubilet–Rota–Stein straightening identities.

## Limitations

The current artifact does not formalize:

- the affine rank-`≤ r` matrix variety;
- its vanishing ideal or its geometric identification with `Jr`;
- reducedness of the displayed Gröbner basis;
- the complete multigraded Hilbert-function statement from row and column
  content (the public final chain is total-degree graded);
- a public arbitrary-commutative-ring straightening API.

These are limitations, not implemented future features.
