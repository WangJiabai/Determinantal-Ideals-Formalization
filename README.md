# Determinantal Ideals in Lean 4

This repository is a Lean 4 / Mathlib formalization project on generic minors, determinantal ideals, and Gröbner bases of determinantal ideals.

The project studies a generic `m × n` matrix over the polynomial ring

```lean
MvPolynomial (Fin m × Fin n) k
```

and aims to formalize the classical theorem that minors of a fixed size form a Gröbner basis under a suitable diagonal or anti-diagonal term order.

## Current status

The project has progressed beyond the foundational API stage. The main combinatorial component of the Gröbner basis proof has now been formalized:

* the KRS correspondence between monomial exponent vectors and standard Young bitableaux;
* degree preservation under the KRS correspondence;
* the Schensted-type equality identifying monomial width with tableau length;
* the resulting theorem `exists_krsEquiv`.

The dependency audit for the completed KRS theorem gives:

```lean
'Determinantal.exists_krsEquiv' depends on axioms:
[propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound]
```

In particular, this completed KRS chain does not depend on `sorryAx`.

At present, the only remaining `sorry` in the current development is the straightening-law theorem:

```lean
straightening_law
```

This theorem is the remaining major ingredient needed to complete the standard-bitableau and Hilbert-function route to the determinantal Gröbner basis theorem.

## Formalized components

The repository currently contains formalizations of the following material.

### Generic minors and determinantal ideals

* generic matrices over `MvPolynomial`;
* index data for minors;
* generic minor polynomials;
* sets of fixed-size minors;
* determinantal ideals generated by minors;
* finiteness and finite-generation results;
* evaluation at concrete matrices;
* compatibility under coefficient-ring homomorphisms.

### Determinant terms and term orders

* signs and permutation terms in determinant expansions;
* exponent vectors of diagonal and anti-diagonal determinant terms;
* diagonal and anti-diagonal term-order predicates;
* leading-term results for minors under suitable term-order hypotheses.

### Young bitableaux and KRS correspondence

* Young bitableaux and standard Young bitableaux;
* polynomial interpretation of bitableaux as products of minors;
* generalized permutations and monomial width;
* row insertion and reverse row insertion;
* recording tableaux and certified KRS forward runs;
* reverse KRS output ordering;
* forward/reverse KRS inverse theorems;
* KRS packaged as a Lean `Equiv`;
* Schensted's theorem relating longest decreasing subsequences to first-column height;
* degree- and width-preserving correspondence between monomial exponent vectors and standard Young bitableaux.

### Gröbner basis proof route

The project develops the standard-monomial/KRS route toward the determinantal Gröbner basis theorem:

```text
KRS correspondence
  + straightening law
  + standard-bitableau counting / Hilbert-function comparison
  + leading terms of minors
  ⇒ Gröbner basis theorem for determinantal ideals
```

The KRS and Schensted components of this route are now complete. The straightening law remains under formalization.

The development is organized into reusable foundational APIs, the active Sturmfels/KRS proof route, and an older experimental Gröbner-basis route.

| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| `Finite.lean`         | Establishes `Finite`/`Fintype` instances for `MinorIndex` and proves that the set of all fixed-size generic minors is finite.                                                                                                                                   |

| `Eval.lean`           | Defines minors of concrete matrices and the evaluation algebra homomorphism from the generic polynomial ring; proves that evaluating a generic minor yields the corresponding concrete minor.                                                                   |
| `ConcreteMatrix.lean` | Proves naturality of concrete matrix minors under coefficient-ring homomorphisms: applying a ring map to a minor agrees with taking the minor after mapping the matrix entries.                                                                                 |
| `BaseChange.lean`     | Proves compatibility of the generic matrix, generic minors, and determinantal ideals with change of coefficient ring.                                                                                                                                           |
| `Sign.lean`           | Provides determinant sign lemmas for row and column permutations and packages the square submatrix associated with a minor index as a bridge to `genericMinor`.                                                                                                 |

## Main completed milestone: KRS correspondence

A central completed result is the existence of an equivalence

```lean
((Fin m × Fin n) →₀ ℕ) ≃ StandardYoungBitableau m n
```

which preserves total degree and identifies monomial width with Young-bitableau length.

Conceptually, the construction factors through:

```text
monomial exponent vector
  ↔ sorted biword / generalized permutation
  ↔ same-shape pair of tableaux via KRS
  ↔ standard Young bitableau
```

The proof includes formalizations of:

* forward row insertion;
* reverse row insertion;
* legality of recording-tableau updates;
* ordering of reverse KRS output;
* both inverse directions of the KRS algorithm;
* the Schensted correspondence between longest decreasing subsequences and first-column height.

## Remaining work

The principal remaining theorem is the straightening law for Young bitableaux:

```lean
theorem straightening_law ...
```

Its intended role is to prove that every Young-bitableau polynomial has a unique expansion as a finite linear combination of standard Young-bitableau polynomials, preserving degree and respecting the length filtration needed for determinantal ideals.

The current intended proof source is Richard G. Swan's paper:

> *On the Straightening Law for Minors of a Matrix*

The expected formalization route is:

1. formalize a two-minor straightening relation;
2. recursively straighten arbitrary products of minors;
3. establish the degree and length filtration properties;
4. prove uniqueness of standard expansions, preferably reusing the existing KRS and leading-term infrastructure where possible.
